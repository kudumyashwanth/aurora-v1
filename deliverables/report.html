Aurora v1 — AI SoC

A complete System-on-Chip taken from RTL to a signed-off GDSII layout, using only open-source tools, on a single 23 GB laptop.
Engineering report · Yashwanth Kudum · 2026

1. Executive summary

Aurora v1 is a multi-clock AI System-on-Chip integrating a 64-bit RISC-V control core with a hardware matrix-multiply accelerator over a coherent-terminated AXI4 fabric. It was carried through the entire physical-design flow — RTL, lint, simulation, formal, synthesis, floorplan, placement, clock-tree, routing, timing, DRC, LVS — to a manufacturable GDSII layout on the SkyWater Sky130B process. Final sign-off: timing MET across all process corners, DRC = 1 (fill waiver), and a device-perfect LVS match.

RTL→GDSII complete  ·  137,562 devices  ·  ~1.97 M instances  ·  41.25 mm² die  ·  33/50 MHz multi-clock

2. Architecture

A Rocket RV64IMAC tile drives the fabric through dual AXI4 masters (cacheable memory + uncached MMIO), clock-crossed from the 33 MHz CPU domain into the 50 MHz fabric. An 8×8 round-robin AXI4 crossbar routes to a boot ROM, on-chip SRAM, UART, GPIO, timer, and the tensor accelerator.

5. Functional verification

The full SoC boots in simulation: the Rocket core comes up through the real fabric, initializes operand matrices, launches the tensor core, polls for completion, reads the result back over AXI, and reaches its run loop — zero traps.

6. Engineering highlights

  • Cracked a routing-congestion wall on the dense systolic datapath — diagnosed a metal-1 short-storm and beat it with a soft per-layer routing-resource derate.
  • Hardened a RISC-V tile as an AXI macro — re-wrapped Rocket's TileLink-C port through a cache cork + TL-to-AXI4 bridge so it presents a clean AXI4 master.
  • Full-chip PDN/LVS integration — root-caused a power-grid fragmentation (a macro exposing power on two metal layers) and fixed it with a PDN keep-out so the power net extracts as one node.
  • Memory-bounded P&R — the entire flow runs capped at 18 GB to survive on a 23 GB host, with resume-from-checkpoint to iterate the heavy steps.
